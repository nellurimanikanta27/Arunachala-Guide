Design a spiritually immersive mobile application UI/UX for an Arunachala Girivalam companion app. The app should not feel like a travel app, fitness tracker, or temple information directory. Instead, it should evoke the feeling of entering a sacred space and walking alongside Arunachala as a silent spiritual companion.

The overall design philosophy should be: "Sacred Fire in Stillness."

The visual experience must draw inspiration from Lord Shiva as Arunachala, representing the eternal Agni (Fire) Lingam. This is not a fierce or destructive fire, but a calm, transformative inner flame symbolizing awareness, surrender, and spiritual awakening.

The ambience should communicate:

* Stillness and peace.
* Sacred warmth and devotion.
* Timelessness rooted in ancient tradition.
* Emotional intimacy and reflection.
* Quiet guidance rather than instruction.

The emotional journey of the user should unfold as:
Arrival → Presence → Reflection → Transformation → Calling Back.

Design the interface using a refined, minimal aesthetic inspired by:

* Arunachala Hill at sunrise and sunset.
* Traditional South Indian temple architecture.
* The soft glow of oil lamps.
* Stone textures found in ancient temples.
* Sacred ash (vibhuti), bronze, sandalwood, and firelight.

Color palette:

* Deep Charcoal: #151515
* Warm Stone: #F4EFE6
* Sacred Ember Orange: #D46A1E
* Temple Bronze: #8B6A3F
* Lamp Glow Gold: #F7D98B
* Primary Text (Dark Mode): #F7F4ED
* Secondary Text: #C7BFAF

Typography:

* Elegant timeless serif fonts for headings, conveying wisdom and reverence.
* Clean modern sans-serif fonts for body text, ensuring readability and accessibility.
* The design should balance ancient spirituality with modern usability.

The home screen should immediately create a sacred emotional connection. It should feature a subtle silhouette of Arunachala Hill, soft ambient lighting effects, and the greeting:

"Om Arunachaleswaraya Namah"
"The Hill that calls."

The primary action should invite the user into the experience with a button such as:
"Begin Today's Journey."

The Girivalam map experience must not resemble conventional navigation systems. Avoid harsh blue routes and standard GPS aesthetics. Instead:

* The walking path should appear as a gently glowing sacred thread encircling Arunachala.
* Visited Lingams should emit a warm illuminated presence.
* Upcoming Lingams should feel inviting rather than task-oriented.
* Arunachala Hill should remain visually dominant and spiritually central.

At each Lingam, the interface should encourage contemplation rather than documentation. Replace generic prompts such as "Upload Photo" with emotionally resonant interactions such as:

"Pause for a moment."
"How would you like to remember this blessing?"

Options may include:

* Capture a photo.
* Record a voice reflection.
* Save a prayer.
* Express a feeling.
* Write a journal note.

The memory experience should transform pilgrimage moments into a beautiful spiritual timeline rather than a social media feed.

The completion experience should feel profound and ceremonial. Upon completing Girivalam:

* The screen should gradually brighten.
* Subtle temple bells should accompany the transition.
* Arunachala Hill should emerge through warm light.
* The interface should communicate a sense of gratitude, accomplishment, and inner peace.

Display a message such as:

"You walked."
"You reflected."
"You surrendered."
"Arunachala walked with you."

Follow this with a sacred summary including:

* Distance walked.
* Lingams visited.
* Personal reflections collected.
* Spiritual memories preserved.

End with:

"Until the Hill calls again."

The interface should feel inclusive across generations:

* Supportive and simple for elderly devotees.
* Meaningful and reflective for adults.
* Visually authentic and emotionally engaging for younger users.
* Respectful of tradition for lifelong devotees.

Avoid:

* Gamification elements.
* Excessive notifications.
* Social media aesthetics.
* Bright modern gradients.
* Neon colors.
* Overly commercial design language.
* Cluttered dashboards.

The final experience should feel like a harmonious fusion of:

* A temple's serenity.
* A trusted pilgrimage companion's practicality.
* A private journal's intimacy.
* The quiet presence of Shiva guiding the journey.

The user should leave the app feeling:
"I did not merely use an application. I experienced Arunachala."
