Build the Sadhana feature for Arunachala Guide.

This feature is not a content section. It is a daily spiritual practice system that helps users build discipline, peace, clarity, and inner strength.

Core purpose:
Help pilgrims carry Arunachala into everyday life through daily spiritual practice.

The Sadhana section should feel:
Sacred, calm, simple, non-commercial, respectful, personal, and easy for all users including elderly users and first-time spiritual practitioners.

Do not make it look like a fitness tracker.
Do not make it feel like a productivity app.
Do not use aggressive streak pressure or social media style gamification.
The experience should feel like a gentle spiritual companion.

Main screen:
The screen should open with a peaceful white or warm ash background with gold accents.
At the top, show:
- Greeting: “Begin where you are.”
- Short supporting line: “Even five minutes of practice can change the quality of your day.”
- Current daily streak
- Total practice minutes
- This week’s practice count
- A soft reminder: “No pressure. Return gently.”

Primary CTA:
“Start Today’s Sadhana”

Practice categories:
1. Silent Meditation
2. Self-Enquiry
3. Japa
4. Pranayama
5. Silence Practice
6. Walking Meditation
7. Chanting
8. Arunachala Remembrance

Each practice should include:
- Name
- Short meaning
- Recommended duration
- Beginner friendly guidance
- Start button
- Optional audio guidance
- Save completion action

Practice duration options:
- 5 minutes
- 10 minutes
- 20 minutes
- 30 minutes
- 60 minutes

Practice detail screen:
When a user selects a practice, show a clean practice screen with:
- Practice name
- Short explanation
- “How to do this” steps
- Duration selector
- Start button
- Optional guidance toggle
- Calm visual breathing or stillness animation

Example for Self-Enquiry:
Title: “Self-Enquiry”
Subtitle: “Turn attention back to the one who is aware.”
Steps:
1. Sit comfortably.
2. Let the breath settle.
3. When a thought appears, ask: “To whom has this thought come?”
4. Notice the feeling of “I”.
5. Rest there without trying to answer intellectually.

Timer screen:
When practice starts, open a focused full-screen experience.
The timer screen should be minimal:
- Large calm timer
- Practice name
- Soft breathing circle or gentle golden light animation
- Pause button
- End session button
- No clutter
- No notifications
- No unnecessary stats during practice

For meditation:
Show a slowly breathing golden circle.

For japa:
Show the mantra gently:
“Arunachala Shiva”
or
“Om Namah Shivaya”

For self-enquiry:
Show gentle prompts:
“Who am I?”
“To whom does this thought arise?”
“Rest as awareness.”

For pranayama:
Show breathing phases:
“Inhale”
“Hold”
“Exhale”
Use a slow expanding and contracting circle.

After practice completion:
Show a peaceful completion screen:
- “Practice Complete”
- Duration completed
- Practice name
- Gentle message: “You returned to yourself today.”
- Optional reflection prompt
- Save to My Arunachala
- Continue button

Reflection after Sadhana:
Optional prompts:
- What changed after practice?
- What disturbed your peace today?
- What did you notice within?
- What are you grateful for?
- What will you carry into the rest of the day?

Daily routine builder:
Allow users to create simple routines:
- Morning Sadhana
- Evening Sadhana
- Before sleep
- During stress
- Before Girivalam
- After Girivalam

Routine examples:
5 minute routine:
- 1 minute silence
- 3 minutes japa
- 1 minute gratitude

10 minute routine:
- 2 minutes breathing
- 5 minutes meditation
- 3 minutes self-enquiry

20 minute routine:
- 5 minutes pranayama
- 10 minutes meditation
- 5 minutes reflection

60 minute routine:
- 10 minutes pranayama
- 20 minutes meditation
- 20 minutes self-enquiry
- 10 minutes silence

Progress tracking:
Track gently:
- Current streak
- Total practice days
- Total minutes practiced
- Favorite practice
- Weekly consistency
- Monthly practice history

Do not overemphasize failure.
If a user misses a day, show:
“Begin again gently.”
Not:
“You lost your streak.”

My Sadhana archive:
Save:
- Completed practices
- Reflections
- Favorite mantras
- Practice notes
- Personal insights
- Monthly progress
- Spiritual milestones

Design style:
Use:
- Warm white background
- Sacred ash tones
- Gold accents
- Soft saffron highlights
- Large readable text
- Rounded corners
- Calm spacing
- Minimal icons
- Gentle animations
- Serif headings for sacred feeling
- Clean sans-serif body text

Avoid:
- Dark gym-style timer UI
- Loud colors
- Too many cards
- Complicated charts
- Competitive badges
- Social sharing pressure
- Addictive gamification

Navigation:
The Sadhana feature should include:
1. Sadhana Home
2. Practice Library
3. Practice Detail
4. Timer Screen
5. Completion Screen
6. Reflection Screen
7. Routine Builder
8. Sadhana History

Sample copy:
Home title:
“Sadhana”

Subtitle:
“Daily practice to carry Arunachala within.”

CTA:
“Start Today’s Sadhana”

Completion message:
“You practiced today. Let the stillness stay with you.”

Missed day message:
“Begin again. Arunachala does not count your failures.”

Final feeling:
The user should feel:
“This app is helping me build a real spiritual life, not just consume spiritual content.”
